Why Choose Canada?

Why Study in Canada?

Canada has emerged as a leading destination for international students, attracting over 800,000 students from around the globe. Its reputation is bolstered by a combination of high-quality education, diverse cultural experiences, and pathways to permanent residency after graduation.

Quality Education

Canadian institutions are known for their high academic standards and wide range of programs at designated learning institutions (DLIs) across the country. The education system emphasizes research and innovation.

Affordable Costs

Canada offers more affordable tuition compared to countries like the US or UK (about 27% lower than US tuition). Living costs vary by city, with Montreal being particularly affordable with student housing around $550 CAD/month.

Work Opportunities

Students can work while studying and apply for a Post-Graduation Work Permit after graduation, gaining valuable Canadian work experience that can lead to permanent residency.

Safe Environment

Canada is known for its safe, inclusive society and welcoming attitude towards international students, making adaptation easier.

Industry Connections

Canadian universities collaborate closely with industries, promoting research and development that benefits students through real-world applications.

Quality of Life

With stunning natural landscapes, vibrant cities, and strong healthcare, Canada consistently ranks high for quality of life.

Cost of Studying in Canada

As an international student, it is very important to consider the estimated cost of tuition and general living expenses before making a decision. Plan your finances carefully to ensure you can comfortably cover your expenses while studying in Canada.

Tuition Fees

Whether undergraduate or postgraduate programs, international students should budget between:

$20,000 CAD - $30,000 CAD per year

This varies significantly depending on the program and institution.

Living Expenses

The average living expenses for international students typically range from:

$15,000 CAD - $20,000 CAD per year

(excluding tuition fees)

Breakdown of Expenses

Expense Category Estimated Cost Notes
Accommodation $500 - $1,500 CAD/month Depends on city and housing type (on-campus, shared, private)
Food $200 - $600 CAD/month Varies by dietary preferences and cooking habits
Transportation $80 - $120 CAD/month Public transportation costs vary by city
Miscellaneous $200 - $400 CAD/month Personal expenses, entertainment, etc.

Admission Intakes in Canada

Canada typically offers three major admission intakes for international students:

Fall Intake

Starts in September. This is the most popular intake with the majority of programs beginning during this period and the most students applying.

September Start

Winter Intake

Occurs in January/February. Provides an opportunity for students who missed fall intake or are looking for specific winter-start programs.

January Start

Summer Intake

Less common, usually starts in May. Primarily for specialized courses that may not be available during other intakes.

May Start
