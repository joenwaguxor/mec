Study in France

World-class education in the world's 4th most popular study destination

Why Choose France?

Why Study in France?

France offers an enriching experience that extends beyond academics, with over 250,000 international students choosing it each year.

High-Quality Education

Home to world's top universities and grandes écoles with rigorous standards and innovative research. Many programs offered in English for international students.

Affordable Tuition

Public universities charge only €170-€10,000/year. Much lower than other Western countries, with scholarships available for international students.

Cultural Richness

Immerse in France's art, architecture, cuisine and fashion. Explore historical landmarks, museums and local traditions while you study.

Diverse Community

4th most popular study destination attracts students worldwide, creating multicultural campuses and global networking opportunities.

Work Opportunities

Students can work part-time (20 hrs/week). Strong job market in technology, engineering and hospitality for graduates.

Travel Hub

Central European location with excellent transport links. Easily explore neighboring countries during breaks.

Cost of Studying in France

As an international student, it's important to consider the estimated cost before making a decision.

Tuition Fees

Institution Type Annual Fees (EUR)
Public Universities (Bachelor's) €170 - €650
Public Universities (Master's) €243 - €10,000
Private Universities €1,500 - €20,000
Grandes Écoles €500 - €30,000

Most public universities offer very affordable rates compared to private institutions.

Living Expenses

The average living expenses for international students in France typically range from €600 to €1,400 per month (€7,200-€16,800/year).

Expense Monthly Cost (EUR)
Accommodation €300 - €800
Food & Groceries €200 - €400
Transportation €30 - €70
Health Insurance €40 - €80
Miscellaneous €100 - €200
Paris costs ~30% more than other French cities

Admission Intakes in France

French universities typically offer two main admission intakes throughout the academic year

September Intake

The primary intake accommodating most undergraduate and postgraduate programs. Offers the widest range of courses with application deadlines typically in May/June.

Main Intake Wide Course Selection

January Intake

The secondary intake with fewer program options, often for specific courses. Application deadlines usually in October/November of previous year.

Secondary Intake Limited Programs

Application Timeline

We encourage you to contact us at least 6 to 9 months before your desired intake to allow ample time for professional advice and preparation.

Student Visa Process

International students wishing to study in France will need to obtain a student visa after receiving admission from an accredited institution.

Our Visa Support

Our visa experts will simplify the French student visa process, ensuring your application is complete and error-free.

We guide you through the entire process until you secure your visa, including:

  • Campus France registration
  • Financial proof preparation
  • Health insurance guidance
  • Visa interview coaching
