Study in India

Affordable world-class education in a culturally rich environment

Why Choose India?

Why Study in India?

India has emerged as a popular study destination for international students, offering a unique blend of rich history, diverse culture, and a wide range of academic programs.

Quality Education

India is home to numerous prestigious institutions that provide high-quality education. Many universities are recognized globally and have a strong emphasis on research and innovation.

Affordable Costs

One of the significant advantages of studying in India is the affordability. Tuition fees are generally lower compared to Western countries, and the cost of living is also quite reasonable.

Cultural Experience

Studying in India provides students with an opportunity to immerse themselves in a vibrant culture. The country is known for its diverse traditions, languages, and cuisines.

Academic Programs

India boasts a variety of academic programs across numerous fields, including engineering, medicine, arts, and social sciences. Many universities offer courses in English.

Shorter Degrees

Typically, a bachelor's degree in India can be completed in about three years, which is shorter than in many Western countries.

Welcoming Environment

International students often find India to be a friendly and welcoming place. The local population is eager to learn about different cultures.

Cost of Studying in India

As an international student, it is very important to consider the estimated cost of tuition and general living expenses before making a decision.

Tuition Fees

The average tuition fees for international students studying in India typically range from $3,300 to $7,800 per academic year. This range can vary significantly based on factors such as the university, the specific course of study, and the location of the institution.

Program Annual Fees (USD)
Undergraduate Programs $1,175 - $5,800
Postgraduate Programs $3,300 - $7,800
Medical Degrees $4,000 - $15,000

Living Expenses

The average living expenses for international students studying in India typically range from $2,500 to $3,500 per year. This estimate includes various costs such as accommodation, food, transportation, and other personal expenses.

Expense Monthly Cost (USD) Annual Cost (USD)
Accommodation $100 - $300 $1,200 - $3,600
Food $100 - $200 $1,200 - $2,400
Transportation $20 - $50 $240 - $600
Miscellaneous $50 - $100 $600 - $1,200

Admission Intakes in India

International students typically have 2 main admission intakes each year

July Intake

This is the primary intake for most undergraduate and postgraduate programs. Many universities begin their academic year in July, making it a popular choice for international students.

Main Intake

January Intake

Some universities also offer a secondary intake in January. This intake is less common but is available for certain programs, particularly in fields like management and some postgraduate courses.

Secondary Intake

Application Timeline

Student Visa Process

International students wishing to study in India will need to obtain a student visa. However before applying for a student visa prospective students must have received admission offer from an accredited institution.
