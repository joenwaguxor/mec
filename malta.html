Study in Malta

Experience Mediterranean education in Europe's sunniest destination

Why Choose Malta?

Why Study in Malta?

Malta offers a unique blend of Mediterranean lifestyle, English-speaking education, and rich cultural heritage

European Education Standards

As part of the European Higher Education Area, Malta offers internationally recognized degrees aligned with European standards.

English-Speaking

English is an official language, making it easy for international students to adapt and communicate effectively.

Rich History

Explore 7,000 years of history with Neolithic temples, medieval cities, and Baroque architecture at your doorstep.

Safe Environment

Ranked as one of the safest countries in the world with a low crime rate and welcoming community.

Mediterranean Climate

Enjoy over 300 days of sunshine annually with mild winters and warm summers perfect for outdoor activities.

Diverse Community

Join a multicultural student body from across Europe, Africa, and beyond in a truly international setting.

Affordable Living

Lower costs compared to mainland Europe with reasonable tuition fees and living expenses.

Work Opportunities

International students can work part-time while studying to gain experience and offset costs.

Island Lifestyle

Stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant nightlife create an exceptional student experience.

Cost of Studying in Malta

As an international student, it's important to consider the estimated cost before making a decision.

Tuition Fees (EUR)

Program Type Annual Fees
Bachelor's Degrees €7,000 - €12,000
Master's Degrees €7,000 - €18,000
Specialized Programs Up to €24,000
Language Courses €1,500 - €5,000 (per course)
Fees vary by institution and program - contact us for exact figures

Living Expenses

The average living expenses for international students typically range from €660 to €1,320 per month (€7,920-€15,840/year).

Expense Monthly Cost (EUR)
Accommodation €300-€600 (shared more affordable)
Food €200-€300
Transportation €26 (monthly pass)
Miscellaneous €100-€200

More affordable than most Western European destinations

Admission Intakes in Malta

Malta universities typically offer two main admission intakes per year

October Intake

The primary intake beginning in October, when most programs commence.

Main Intake Most Programs

February Intake

The secondary intake beginning in February for selected programs.

Secondary Intake Limited Programs

Application Timeline

We encourage you to contact us at least 10 to 12 months before your desired intake to allow ample time for professional advice and preparation.

Student Visa Process

International students wishing to study in Malta will need to obtain a student visa after receiving admission from an accredited institution.
