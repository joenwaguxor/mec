Why Choose Netherlands?

Why Study in the Netherlands?

The Netherlands offers a unique blend of high-quality education, cultural diversity, and a welcoming environment

Quality Education

The Dutch higher education system is known for its international approach and high standards, with many universities ranked among the top globally.

English-Taught Programs

Wide range of English-taught programs available, making it accessible for international students without Dutch language proficiency.

Cultural Diversity

Home to students from over 160 countries, creating a multicultural environment that enriches the educational experience.

Scholarships Available

Various scholarships like the Holland Scholarship (€5,000) and university-specific awards help reduce financial burden.

Affordable Costs

Lower tuition fees and living costs compared to other English-speaking countries while maintaining high education standards.

Excellent Infrastructure

Outstanding transportation system and high quality of life with focus on sustainability and student well-being.

Innovative Teaching

Modern, interactive teaching methods that emphasize problem-solving and practical application of knowledge.

Strategic Location

Heart of Europe location provides easy access to travel and explore other European countries during studies.

Open Society

Dutch culture is known for openness, tolerance, and direct communication, creating a welcoming environment.

Cost of Studying in the Netherlands

As an international student, it's important to consider the estimated cost before making a decision.

Tuition Fees (EUR)

Student Type Annual Fees
Non-EU International Students €6,000 - €20,000
EU/EEA Students (Statutory Fee) ~€2,530

Fees vary by institution and program level (bachelor's/master's)

Living Expenses

The average living expenses for international students typically range from €800 to €1,200 per month (€9,600-€14,400/year).

Expense Monthly Cost (EUR)
Housing €300 - €700
Food €200 - €300
Transportation €50 - €100
Miscellaneous €150 - €300
Amsterdam costs ~20-30% more than other Dutch cities

Admission Intakes in the Netherlands

Dutch universities typically offer two main admission intakes per year

Fall Intake (September/October)

The primary intake beginning in September/October, aligning with the academic year start. Most programs available with deadlines typically in April/May.

Main Intake Most Programs

Spring Intake (February/March)

The secondary intake beginning in February/March for selected programs. Application deadlines typically October/November.

Secondary Intake Limited Programs

Application Timeline

We encourage you to contact us at least 6 to 9 months before your desired intake to allow ample time for professional advice and preparation.

Student Visa Process

International students wishing to study in the Netherlands will need to obtain a residence permit after receiving admission from an accredited institution.
