Why Choose UK?

Why Study in UK?

The UK is a top destination for international students, known for its rich culture, diverse educational offerings, and high-quality institutions. It ranks as the second most popular study destination worldwide.

World-Class Institutions

The UK is home to over 130 universities, offering a wide range of courses across various fields with access to some of the world's leading educators and researchers.

Academic Excellence

UK universities are consistently ranked among the best globally, ensuring degrees are recognized and respected worldwide, enhancing graduates' employability.

Strong Career Prospects

Graduates from UK universities are among the most employable in the world, with degrees that significantly boost career opportunities and earning potential.

Rich Culture

Famous for its arts, history and vibrant cities, the UK offers an unforgettable cultural experience alongside academic benefits.

Student Support

The UK provides excellent support for international students, including orientation programs and services to help adapt to life in the UK.

English Language

Studying in an English-speaking country provides an immersive environment to enhance language skills, a valuable asset in the global job market.

Cost of Studying in UK

As an international student, it is very important to consider the estimated cost of tuition and general living expenses before making a decision.

Tuition Fees

Tuition fees vary significantly based on the level of study and institution:

  • Undergraduate: £11,400 - £38,000 per year
  • Postgraduate: £12,000 - £40,000 per year
  • Medical degrees: Often at higher end of spectrum

Living Expenses

Estimated annual living costs (including accommodation, food, transportation):

  • London: £15,600 - £16,800 per year
  • Outside London: £10,800 - £15,600 per year
  • Monthly average: £900 - £1,400 depending on location

Admission Intakes in UK

The UK has three main intakes for international students:

Autumn Intake

September start - The primary and most popular intake with around 80% of international students applying during this period when most courses are available.

September Start

Winter Intake

January start - Offers another opportunity for students who may have missed September intake. Less common but widely available across many universities.

January Start

Summer Intake

May start - The least common intake available for a limited number of courses and universities, providing flexibility for alternative start dates.

May Start
