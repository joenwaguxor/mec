Why Choose USA?

Why Study in USA?

The United States is one of the most popular destinations for international students, known for its diverse educational opportunities and vibrant campus life.

World-Class Education

Home to many of the world's top universities, with over 151 institutions ranked among global leaders in academic standards and innovative research.

Flexible Education System

Offers diverse programs from undergraduate to professional degrees with research and training opportunities that can provide funding through positions like Teaching Assistants.

Cultural Diversity

Experience a unique cultural melting pot with influences from around the world, offering invaluable personal growth and global perspectives.

Career Opportunities

Abundant internships, research positions, and networking through university-industry connections crucial for career development.

Student Support

Well-organized support system including academic advising, counseling services, and international student offices to help you adapt.

Recognized Degrees

Degrees from U.S. institutions are highly regarded worldwide, enhancing job prospects and career advancement opportunities.

Cost of Studying in USA

As an international student, it is very important to consider the estimated cost of tuition and general living expenses before making a decision.

Tuition Fees

Tuition costs vary by institution type and program:

  • Public Universities: $15,000 - $25,000 per year
  • Private Colleges: $20,000 - $30,000 per year
  • General Range: $25,000 - $55,000 per year
  • Many institutions offer scholarships and assistantships

Living Expenses

Estimated annual living costs (including housing, food, transportation):

  • Annual Range: $10,000 - $25,000 per year
  • Monthly Average: $1,000 - $2,000 per month
  • Housing: ~60% of total living costs
  • Costs vary significantly between urban and rural areas

Admission Intakes in USA

The United States has three major admission intakes for international students each year:

Fall Intake

Primary intake starting in August/September with the highest number of admissions and most popular choice among students.

August/September Start

Spring Intake

Second most common option starting in January for students who missed fall intake or prefer winter start.

January Start

Summer Intake

Less common intake starting around May, offered by fewer institutions compared to fall and spring.

May Start

Application Timeline

We encourage prospective students to contact us at least 1 year before the start of an intake to allow ample time for professional advice and stress-free preparation.

Student Visa Process

Visa consideration at the US embassy is done case by case at the discretion of the Visa Officer during your interview.
